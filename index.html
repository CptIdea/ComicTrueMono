How it was made

    
Assembled from unmerged pull requests.

    
Good contributions to the free monospaced Comic Sans forks sat unmerged for years вҖ”
      the original Comic Shanns went quiet in 2023, and even the maintained repos still ignore font PRs
      (kaBeech's italics have been open since 2024). Comic True Mono gathers that scattered work into one
      place вҖ” every glyph from a free, redistributable source вҖ” and constructs the few that existed nowhere.

The fine print

    
Why it's OFL.

    

      
No philosophy here вҖ” it just shook out this way. Every glyph used to come from an
        MIT source, not on principle but because that's simply what the free Comic Sans
        forks happened to be.

      
Then the font needed Cyrillic and Greek, and the only free, comic-styled set of those lives
        in one place: Comic Relief, which is OFL. The license is
        contagious вҖ” build on its outlines and your font ships under it too.

      
So the fonts are OFL 1.1. The build scripts stay MIT, since they hold no outlines
        but their own.

      
That's the whole story вҖ” nobody here was guarding a license.
